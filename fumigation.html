Fumigation Process

Fumigation is a pest control method that involves the use of gaseous pesticides, known as fumigants, to eliminate pests within an enclosed space or structure. It is commonly employed to control a wide range of pests, including termites, bedbugs, stored product pests, and other insect infestations.

Fumigation Process

Malabar Pest Control has been a fumigation expert for almost 16 years. Here is our steps on perfoming the fumigation process
01

Preparation

The area to be fumigated needs to be carefully prepared. This may involve sealing off the space, removing or covering food items, plants, and sensitive materials, and ensuring the area is adequately ventilated.

02

Application

Fumigants are introduced into the enclosed space in the form of gas, liquid, or solid materials. The fumigant disperses throughout the area, reaching all infested areas and targeting the pests.

03

Exposure Time

The duration of fumigation varies depending on the target pest, the size of the space, and the fumigant being used. It is critical to ensure that the required exposure time is maintained for effective pest eradication.

04

Aeration

After the exposure period, the fumigant is ventilated or aerated from the space to make it safe for re-entry. Aeration may involve the use of fans, air circulation, and testing to ensure the air quality meets safety standards.

Fumigation can be an effective solution for eliminating stubborn infestations and pests that are difficult to reach using conventional methods. However, it is important to consult with professional pest control services to determine if fumigation is the appropriate and necessary method for your specific pest problem.
